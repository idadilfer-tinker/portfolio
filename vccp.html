Context

Design leadership across three regulated industries at once.

At VCCP I led product design across three regulated industries at once — healthcare, pensions, and financial services — for Walby, Pension Buddy, and Cambridge Building Society, translating complex products into intuitive, accessible digital experiences used by millions.

Across all three products, I delivered core components and a shared library framework that met WCAG 2.1 AA — bringing each product to that accessibility standard for the first time. All of it delivered at agency pace: parallel workstreams, continuous deployment, and research-led design decisions across three different platforms simultaneously.

The Engagement

Three clients. One standard. Zero compromises.

At VCCP I held design leadership across three regulated clients — Walby, Pension Buddy, and Cambridge Building Society — designing the digital experiences through which everyday people manage their health journeys, pensions, and life savings.

Research informed every decision. I ran interviews and usability sessions to translate complex pension rules, mortgage decisions, and savings behaviour into single-question flows — proving that accessible design and conversion are not mutually exclusive. All three products met WCAG 2.1 AA.

Progressive disclosure — plain language performance first, full regulated detail available on demand — combined with compliance built into the design sprint cycle, not bolted on at the end.

The Problem Space

Each client believed their problem was unique. Most weren't.

Each client presented a distinct version of the same core challenge — translating regulated complexity into simple, guided flows without sacrificing compliance, trust, or conversion.

For Walby, I identified and resolved a 40% mobile bounce rate and built a product that met WCAG 2.1 AA standards for the first time. For Cambridge Building Society, I created a savings and mortgage experience that gave members clarity on their financial options. For Pension Buddy, I built a multi-platform content design system — clear hierarchy, modular components, and real-time pension rules — that let editorial teams publish at pace without breaking the standard.

My Approach

Research-led decisions across every client simultaneously.

I structured my work around research-led decisions for each client — running separate research programmes for Walby, Pension Buddy, and Cambridge Building Society, synthesising findings against a shared research framework that allowed me to identify patterns across clients and accelerate design decisions.

Across all three products, I used a shared design language platform — a single Figma-based system — that allowed rapid delivery across clients without duplicating work or fragmenting the visual language. Research determined which elements, components, and patterns needed to be client-specific and which could be shared — making fast, confident decisions across parallel workstreams the norm rather than the exception.

Process

The decisions behind the design.

01

Ran multi-client research programmes simultaneously

Led separate research programmes across all three clients — healthcare, pension, and financial services — while building a shared framework that surfaced cross-client patterns and accelerated decisions.

02

Grounded every flow in real financial behaviour

Structured product design around how users actually manage money — pension contributions, savings habits, mortgage decisions — with each flow personalised to the user's own data and its regulatory requirements, so no screen prompted a poor or uninformed decision.

03

Adopted single-question flows across all three platforms

Designed each platform around a single-question flow approach — accessibility-compliant, single-question decision flows that reduced cognitive load at every step across all three clients.

04

Designed adaptive components with clear regulations

Designed platform-wide component systems with regulatory adaptations built in — accessibility standards, disclosure requirements, and financial regulation baked in from the first component, not retrofitted.

05

Shared cross-client platform components and patterns

Structured a shared Figma system across all three clients — identifying components that could be shared without compromising client-specific brand standards or regulatory requirements.

06

Adapted quality assurance from other regulated organisations

Implemented a QA process modelled on regulated-industry practice — validating every design decision against regulatory requirements, WCAG standards, and conversion data before release.
