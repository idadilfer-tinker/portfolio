Context

Taking the taboo out of looking after yourself.

Cosmetic surgery — and treatments as everyday as botox — have lived behind closed doors: whispered about, quietly judged, priced opaquely. Oia exists to change that. Patients tell Oia what they long for in their own words; SmartMatch finds surgeons whose verified real-world results most closely resemble the patient's desired outcome; and Oia negotiates the price and package on the patient's behalf.

Matching is domestic-first: Oia matches patients with services in their own country, and offers cross-border options from its vetted international network — spanning the UK, Germany, Lithuania, Spain, Greece, Turkey and Lebanon — when domestic options exceed the patient's budget. One transparent, all-inclusive estimate. £0 hidden fees.

⭑ Core Product Principle

The UI is Oia.

The entire patient-facing product is the AI agent. On the web and in the app, everything is conversational. Intake, results galleries, shortlists, quotes, negotiation updates, booking, travel itineraries, post-op check-ins — every journey happens inside the conversation with Oia, across web chat and WhatsApp as one continuous thread. If a feature can't be expressed conversationally, it doesn't ship patient-side.

01
Rich responses, not pages

Shortlists, quotes, itineraries, and before/after galleries render as rich cards, carousels, and selection buttons within the conversation. The "personalised shortlist link" opens a conversational view — never a listings page.

02
Structured input inside chat

Selection buttons, list messages, and photo prompts carry the intake and every decision point. Free-text is always available, never required.

03
One thread, every channel

Web chat and WhatsApp are the same conversation with Oia — state and history follow the patient across channels, mid-journey, without repetition.

04
Consent lives in the conversation

Sensitive-imagery confirmation, photo-sharing consent, and offer acceptances are conversational moments with logged, explicit confirmations — designed to be as legally robust as any form.

05
Conversation-first specs

Every feature must be expressed as a conversation — what Oia says, what the patient sees and taps — before it is built. The spec format is the design gate.

06
Conventional UI where it earns it

The principle governs the patient side only. The internal curation and approval cockpit and clinic tooling use conventional UI where that's faster to operate.

Key Design Decisions

Four calls that made the agent trustworthy.

Why is the entire patient UI a conversation?

The tensionMarketplaces default to listings, filters, and comparison tables — and every stakeholder instinct pulled that way.
The callPeople don't shop for surgery the way they shop for flights. They confide, hesitate, and return. A conversation holds ambivalence; a listings page pressures it. The constraint became the spec gate: if it can't be said, it doesn't ship.
OutcomeOne design surface to perfect instead of dozens — and an intake patients describe as "asking Oia things I'd never say out loud in a consultation room."

Why domestic-first when cross-border margins are better?

The tensionThe business model is strongest when patients travel; the honest recommendation is often local.
The callMatch at home first, always. Cross-border appears only when domestic options exceed the patient's budget — presented side by side with real prices for both.
OutcomeTrust as the moat. "Prefer to stay closer to home? The choice is always yours" is a line of product copy that doubles as strategy.

How can consent be robust without a single form?

The tensionSensitive imagery, photo sharing, and offer acceptance legally demand explicit, auditable consent — the natural home of forms.
The callConsent became a designed conversational moment: the gallery arrives blurred, the confirmation is explicit and unambiguous, and every acceptance is timestamped and logged to the patient's privacy record.
OutcomeLegal robustness without breaking the thread — and consent moments that patients actually read.

Why conventional UI for the internal cockpit?

The tensionIf conversation is the product philosophy, shouldn't operations be conversational too?
The callNo. The principle serves patients, not ideology. Curation, clinic approval, and negotiation oversight are high-volume operator tasks — tables, queues, and bulk actions are simply faster.
OutcomeA two-speed design system: a single conversational surface patient-side, a pragmatic cockpit inside — each measured by what it's for.
