Context

One of life's biggest decisions — and one of the hardest to navigate.

Fertility care is high-stakes, deeply personal, and strangely opaque. The information is scattered and clinical, prices swing wildly between clinics and countries with hidden extras buried in the fine print, and each option — testing, egg freezing, IVF, embryo freezing — tends to be researched in its own silo.

People end up stitching answers together from forums, sales calls, and spreadsheets, making months-long, life-shaping choices under real emotional and financial pressure. Journey exists to replace that fog with a clear, honest map.

The Idea

Everything you need to understand, track, and plan — in one place.

Journey brings the whole fertility landscape into a single, calm experience. You start with one simple search, understand what each path involves, see what it truly costs all-in, and compare providers — near home or across borders — before you ever pick up the phone.

Research

Grounded in how people actually navigate fertility.

Before any screens, I invested in understanding the journey itself — the questions people ask, the moments they get stuck, and how opaque, variable pricing quietly shapes the decisions they make.

That research set the product's north star: reduce anxiety by replacing scattered, salesy information with one honest, comparable view. It's why comparison and true cost sit at the centre, and why the tone is warm and unhurried rather than clinical.

Key Design Decisions

The calls that made Journey feel trustworthy.

Why lead with comparison instead of a single clinic?

The tensionMost fertility platforms are effectively lead-gen for one clinic or chain — helpful until incentives diverge from the patient's.
The callPut comparison at the centre. People see options side by side, on their terms, before anyone sells to them.
OutcomeTrust as the product's foundation — the same reason the tone stays warm and unhurried.

Why insist on the true, all-in cost?

The tensionHeadline prices are the norm; the real cost — meds, add-ons, extras — surfaces late and erodes trust.
The callShow the honest, complete figure up front and make options genuinely comparable.
OutcomeCost stops being a source of dread and becomes a clear input to the decision.

Why "Where are you right now?" instead of filters?

The tensionEveryone's journey is different, but filter-first UIs assume people already know the vocabulary.
The callLet people choose the plain-language line that sounds like them; tailor the search behind the scenes.
OutcomeAn entry point that meets uncertainty with warmth, not a wall of controls.

Why make warmth the brand's core job?

The tensionThe category defaults to clinical, cold, and slightly shameful.
The callA brand — name, soft gradient identity, and unhurried voice — that treats fertility as something to feel at home with.
OutcomeAn identity that carries the promise from first impression to every screen.
