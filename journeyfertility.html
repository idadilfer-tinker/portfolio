The Problem

The most expensive guess most people ever make.

Fertility care runs into thousands of pounds and reshapes a life — yet people choose almost blind. Quotes swing wildly between clinics and countries, the real total hides behind a tidy "from" price, and every route — a test, egg freezing, IVF, embryos — sits in its own lonely corner of the web.

So the research happens at midnight: forum threads, pushy discovery calls, half-finished spreadsheets. Journey's whole reason to exist is to turn that scramble into one clear, honest view.

Design Decisions

Four calls that earned people's trust.

Comparison first — why not just recommend a clinic?

The tensionMost fertility sites are quietly a funnel for one clinic or chain — useful right up until their interests part ways with yours.
The callMake comparison the whole point. Options sit side by side, on your terms, before anyone tries to sell.
ResultTrust becomes the foundation — and the reason the tone never rushes or pushes.

Why fight for the true, all-in price?

The tension"From" prices are the norm; the real total — meds, add-ons, storage — lands late and quietly breaks trust.
The callPut the honest, complete figure up front, and make two options genuinely comparable.
ResultMoney stops being the thing people dread and becomes a clear part of the decision.

A plain question instead of filters — why?

The tensionEvery journey is different, but filter-first screens assume you already speak the language of fertility.
The callLet people pick the sentence that sounds like them; do the clever filtering out of sight.
ResultA front door that answers uncertainty with warmth, not a wall of switches.

Why hang so much on warmth?

The tensionThe category leans clinical, cold, and faintly shaming.
The callA brand — name, soft gradient light, unhurried voice — that treats fertility as something to feel at home with.
ResultOne feeling that carries from the first glance to the last screen.

Reflections

Where I'd push it further.

Four honest notes from building it — the calls I'd revisit, and what each one taught me.

01I reached for all four routes too soon
What happenedHolding testing, egg freezing, IVF and embryos in one view early made the first designs feel wide but thin.
What it taught meProve the loop on one route, then repeat the pattern — depth wins trust faster than breadth.
02"True cost" pushed back
What happenedHonest all-in pricing collides with messy, uneven real-world data; early mockups implied a precision the numbers couldn't support.
What it taught meDesign for ranges and clearly-labelled estimates — honesty includes owning the uncertainty.
03Filters crept in early
What happenedThe first way in leaned on filters, quietly assuming people already knew the words for what they needed.
What it taught meAnswer uncertainty in plain language — the "where are you right now?" door came straight out of watching people stall.
04Warmth needed a backbone
What happenedPushed too far, "soft and reassuring" started to feel vague in a place where people need hard facts.
What it taught meWarmth and precision aren't a trade — the tone holds the feeling, the numbers hold the trust.

Brand & Voice

Warm, where the whole category is cold.

Brand is where I'm most at home, and on Journey I ran it end to end — name, wordmark, palette, gradient system, photography and voice. Fertility branding defaults to clinical and faintly shaming; I built the opposite, then wrote it down as a small system so it holds together everywhere.
