Context

A two-sided platform for a high-stakes journey.

Prompted by the growing demand for medical tourism and the inefficiencies cosmetic clinics faced managing international patient enquiries, AIDA was created to streamline patient triage, consultation, and booking through an AI-powered WhatsApp experience.

AIDA is a two-sided platform — patients receive guided, conversational support throughout their journey, while clinics manage conversations, appointments, and escalations through a dedicated dashboard.

The Pivot

When complexity multiplied overnight.

When we pivoted AIDA from a marketplace into two distinct products — a community platform and a clinic-facing AI patient agent — the complexity multiplied overnight. The AI agent was no longer just guiding discovery; it was responsible for triaging patients, handling medical disclosures, managing booking logic, and supporting post-op check-ins across borders.

Every conversation branch carried operational, ethical, and clinical risk.

The Challenge

As we began designing the WhatsApp-native conversation structures, we found ourselves repeating early mistakes: edge cases overlooked, medical nuances buried in linear flows, and decision logic that looked clean in Figma but fragile in real life. We couldn't afford to "learn from production" when real patients and surgeons were involved.

The Solution

I designed an internal simulation tool. It generated diverse patient personas, medical histories, and intent patterns — allowing us to pressure-test conversation logic before release.

We ran structured case studies with clinical teams, mapped escalation triggers, identified branching failures, and redesigned the system around decision safety rather than conversational neatness.

This shifted AIDA from a scripted chatbot into a resilient AI workflow engine — one that clinics could trust with real patients, not just demos.

How it was built

I built the simulation tool using Claude Code, then deployed it locally via Cursor so the whole team could access it without any infrastructure overhead.

What started as a personal workaround became a shared team asset — our engineers, clinical leads, and I now run scenarios through it regularly.

The Escalation Framework

Knowing when the AI should stop talking.

The core safety mechanism of the platform. Every AI turn carries an internal confidence score — when it falls below threshold, or a medical disclosure is detected, the conversation escalates to a human with full context. Watch the confidence meter drop.

Process

Mapping the ecosystem before designing a single screen.

01

Discovery & System Mapping

The project began by mapping the complete ecosystem — patient journeys, clinic workflows, AI decision points, escalation scenarios, and operational requirements — before designing any interface. Research with clinic partners revealed that patients needed guidance and reassurance throughout decision-making, while clinics needed better visibility and control over AI-managed conversations.

02

Patient Journey Design

A mobile-first, WhatsApp-native experience was designed to guide patients through initial enquiry and screening, procedure education, photo uploads and assessment, pricing, consultation booking, and post-consultation follow-up. Testing revealed that patients preferred no extra app or account, and that visual guidance significantly improved confidence during photo uploads.

03

Clinic Dashboard Design

A real-time dashboard was designed to help clinics monitor and manage AI-assisted conversations. Testing revealed that staff needed immediate visibility of escalated conversations, that patient context was critical during AI-to-human handoffs, and that mobile access was essential as many teams managed conversations on the move.

04

AI Escalation Framework

A confidence-based escalation model was developed through multiple iterations: AI handles routine enquiries, confidence scores are surfaced internally, conversations automatically escalate when confidence falls below a safe threshold, and clinics receive full context at handoff. Patients experience a seamless transition without restarting the conversation.

The Clinic Side

A console built for triage, not chat admin.

Clinic staff don't monitor conversations — they monitor risk. The console leads with escalations, surfaces AI confidence per conversation, and gives coordinators full patient context the moment they take over.

Key Design Decisions

Four calls that shaped the product.

Why WhatsApp-native, instead of our own app?

The tensionA dedicated app gave us full UI control; WhatsApp gave us none — no custom components, no persistent navigation, no visual brand surface.
The callMeet patients where they already are. Every additional install or account was a barrier stacked on top of an already anxious decision.
OutcomeNear-zero activation friction — and it forced conversation design discipline that made the AI genuinely better.

Why surface AI confidence to clinics but never to patients?

The tensionTransparency suggested showing patients when they were talking to AI vs. humans — including how sure the AI was.
The callPatients always know they can reach a human; but a visible "58% confident" score would erode trust in a medical context without adding agency.
OutcomeConfidence became an internal safety instrument — driving escalation, not anxiety.

Why build a simulation tool instead of more prototypes?

The tensionFigma prototypes looked clean but couldn't express branching conversational risk — the failures lived in the combinations, not the screens.
The callTest the logic, not the pixels. Generated personas pressure-tested every branch before release.
OutcomeBranching failures caught pre-release; the tool became shared team infrastructure.

Why redesign onboarding before adding features?

The tensionThe roadmap pushed for clinic-facing features; but clinics were taking 4 days to reach their first active conversation.
The callActivation is the product. A nine-step email-and-call setup became a three-step in-product flow.
OutcomeTime-to-first-conversation dropped from 4 days to 6 hours.

Product in Motion

The shipped product, recorded.

Before the Pivot

Where it started.

The original AIDA was a patient-facing marketplace — a place to find, compare, and contact cosmetic clinics. The pivot transformed it into a two-sided AI platform, shifting the product from discovery to guided care.

Where It's Headed

From AIDA to Nia.

After gaining enough clinical partners to validate the model at scale, the product is being rebranded from AIDA to Nia — a name and identity built for the next phase of the platform.

With a growing base of clinical partners now onboard, the next chapter re-creates the marketplace experience from the ground up — connecting patients to vetted clinics with the same AI-assisted confidence that powered the original care journey — alongside a dedicated AI support layer for patients, giving them a guided companion throughout treatment, recovery, and aftercare.

Nia carries the trust and safety principles built into AIDA forward, while expanding the product into a fuller ecosystem: marketplace discovery, clinical partnership tooling, and ongoing patient AI support in one connected experience.
